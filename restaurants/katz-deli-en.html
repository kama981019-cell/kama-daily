Overall Review

We stopped by Katz's during our first weeks in NYC — and yes, it was everything the internet promised. If you've scrolled through any NYC food content, you've almost certainly seen their pastrami sandwich tower up at you from a screen. Seeing it in person is something else entirely.

The short version: it's enormous. Wonderfully, unapologetically enormous. Small eaters should seriously consider splitting one sandwich between two people. Hungry visitors can absolutely go solo — just know what you're getting into.

A quick cheat sheet on the meats: corned beef = salt-cured and boiled, pastrami = spiced, smoked, and steamed, brisket = BBQ-smoked. All three are great, but if it's your first visit, the pastrami is the one.

Atmosphere & Access

Katz's sits in the southern part of Manhattan, in the Lower East Side — not exactly a spot you'd stumble upon on a typical tourist route. You come here with a purpose. That said, it pairs well with a visit to Brooklyn or the Financial District, so it's easy to work into a broader day out.

We arrived just after 11am and still waited about ten minutes in line. On busy days, the queue reportedly snakes out the door. Inside, you have two options: the casual counter-service area near the entrance (no tipping required) or a sit-down restaurant section in the back. We opted for the table seats and took our time — no regrets.

The kitchen energy is something to behold. Freshly sliced brisket, mountains of fries, the clatter of trays. There's a rhythm to this place that's been running for over a century.

Final Thoughts

You'll finish your sandwich thinking "I'm good for a while." Then a few weeks pass, and suddenly you're thinking about it again. That's the Katz's effect. If you're in NYC, it deserves a spot on your list — at least once.

Who Should Go

  • First-timers in NYC who want an authentic deli experience
  • Meat lovers who appreciate a no-compromise, fully-loaded sandwich
  • Anyone looking for that iconic, camera-worthy New York moment
