Overview

The Fulton is a seafood restaurant perched on Pier 17 in Manhattan's Seaport district, helmed by French-born culinary legend Jean-Georges Vongerichten — and notably his first seafood-focused concept among his 35+ restaurants worldwide. We visited for a Restaurant Week dinner course at $60, and the cooking was consistently careful and polished, well beyond what the price might suggest. The highlight was the Roasted Skate Wing — a first for both of us — whose rich, fatty flesh was reminiscent of salmon belly in the best possible way. That said, some dishes landed more strongly than others; the tartare leaned heavily on its dressing, which slightly overshadowed the fish itself.

Atmosphere & Access

Situated on Pier 17 along the East River, The Fulton is one of Manhattan's most scenically located restaurants. At night, the illuminated Brooklyn Bridge fills the floor-to-ceiling glass walls and makes the room feel genuinely special. The outdoor terrace is apparently lovely in summer — a different experience altogether — so the restaurant rewards returning in different seasons. It's also an easy stop to add onto a walk along the waterfront or a visit to the Brooklyn Bridge area.

Who it's for

  • Anyone who wants a memorable dinner with a front-row view of the Brooklyn Bridge at night
  • Diners looking to experience Jean-Georges' cooking at a more accessible price point during Restaurant Week
  • Seafood lovers curious to try lesser-known cuts and catches like skate wing
